Why Flash-VAReason?

Visual-only video reasoning misses important acoustic evidence; ASR-only pipelines lose non-verbal cues. Raw audio helps, but uncompressed tokens are expensive.

Audio Time Fusion

Merges locally redundant audio frames using short-time stationarity, reducing repeated silence, steady background sounds, and sustained phonetic segments.

Budget Control

Anchors the retained token budget to the original audio duration, keeping compression behavior stable across clips with different redundancy levels.

Spatial Dynamic Compression

Ranks global token uniqueness and keeps the most acoustically distinctive tokens, preserving cues that are useful for downstream reasoning.

Cue Preservation

Beyond ASR

Retains prosody, pitch contour, speaker characteristics, and environmental sounds that transcripts cannot carry.

Efficiency

Fewer Tokens

Compresses audio tokens by an order of magnitude while keeping the informative acoustic evidence.

Plug-in Design

No Extra Training

Unifies audio and visual compression under information uniqueness without changing the MLLM architecture.

Core Takeaway

Audio is not a decorative modality in video reasoning. It often carries the missing evidence: off-screen events, speaker emotion, environmental context, and causal triggers.

Flash-VAReason keeps the audio stream useful by asking a simple question at token level: which acoustic tokens are unique enough to matter? The result is a practical visual-audio reasoning pipeline that preserves important cues while remaining fast enough for demo and deployment scenarios.
