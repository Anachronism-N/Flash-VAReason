Preserving Acoustic Cues for Video Reasoning:
An Efficient Uniqueness-Driven Token Compression Framework

Haiwei Xue · Zichao Zhao · Zhiyong Wu
Tsinghua University · The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology · The Chinese University of Hong Kong
Paper Code Demo

Abstract

Effective video reasoning requires the joint understanding of visual and acoustic cues. Existing multimodal large language models often discard audio or rely only on ASR transcripts, losing prosody, environmental sounds, speaker characteristics, and other non-verbal evidence. Directly feeding raw audio tokens into MLLMs preserves richer information, but introduces heavy computational overhead.

We propose Flash-VAReason, a visual-audio video reasoning framework that compresses audio tokens according to information uniqueness. The pipeline consists of Audio Time Fusion, Budget Control, and Spatial Dynamic Compression, reducing audio token count while retaining acoustically distinctive cues. Experiments on CharadesEgo and Ego4D show that acoustic cue preservation improves reasoning quality while maintaining efficient inference.
